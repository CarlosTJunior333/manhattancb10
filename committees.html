The Committee works to increase the public’s level of access to information about the arts in the community; reinforces pride in Harlem’s creativity and cultural expression; encourages the creation of public/private partnerships in order to make public spaces available for local artists to exhibit their work; forms collaborations with art and cultural institutions to enhance the community’s access to the arts; and encourages the use of art in public spaces (e.g. parks, gardens, lobbies, libraries, etc.).

The Committee works to encourage and support existing businesses and future commercial business, understanding that one of the key elements of a thriving community is flourishing businesses and equal opportunity. The Committee works with developers, business improvement districts, merchant associations and government agencies to foster the creation and retention of small, locally based businesses by creating a business friendly environment in Central Harlem that increases the commercial and retail opportunities for our residents. Additionally, the Committee is tasked with reviewing and issuing recommendations on NY State Liquor Authority liquor license applications and the Department of Consumer Affairs Sidewalk Café applications.

Meets the 2nd Thursday of every month at 6:30 pm The meeting agendas are posted online in advance of each meeting (see calendar)

The Committee is committed to working through the relevant governmental and non-governmental agencies and organizations to foster the improvement of educational services to the community and coordinate with our teachers, principals, parents and students to make them aware of their options. The Committee hopes that by being the liaison between educational institutions and local residents that they can serve as an educational forum for our parents, educators and elected officials; provide resources (e.g. educational materials) to assist our children in strengthening their academic skills; and advocate for the preservation, modernization and expansion of library services in the community.

The Committee is charged with the responsibility of advocating for the overall better health of our community by addressing issues and concerns such as health threats and hazardous environmental conditions and by supporting efforts to improve service facilities, such as hospitals, clinics and other treatment centers. The Committee is dedicated to developing and implementing strategies designed to improve the health and quality of life of residents in Central Harlem by providing information on the community health studies and preventative health measures that focus on overall sound physical and mental health practices. The Committee also takes up issues related to disability rights and senior citizen services.

The Land Use Committee fulfills its City Charter-mandated duties through its participation in the New York City Uniform Land Use Review Process (ULURP). Additionally, the Committee reviews and provides feedback on as-of-right projects to optimize community benefits, when possible. As it relates to historic preservation, the Committee is committed to identifying and preserving historic resources in the District. This entails the solicitation of historic resource candidates from the community, support of historic designation applications, investigation of historic resource violations by developers and owners, and the demand for enforcement of preservation policy from relevant agencies.

The purpose of the Committee is to increase public safety in Central Harlem by working collaboratively with local and citywide stakeholders to promote evidence-based and innovative solutions, strategies and tactics to reduce, prevent and deter criminal behavior, with a particular focus on our youth. The Committee does this by facilitating coordination, collaboration, and cooperation between the public and private sectors and non-for profit organizations. By fostering new alliances, coordinating efforts and creating networking opportunities between myriad stakeholders, including local residents, elected officials, law enforcement, education, health, and social service agencies, the Committee aims to enhance, expand and supplement existing efforts to increase public safety in the community. The Committee will identify and mitigate gaps between public, private, and not-for-profit efforts to protect Central Harlem residents who are vulnerable to crime as well as to deter others from engaging in criminal behavior.

The Transportation Committee advocates for safe mobility and transportation infrastructure in Central Harlem. Working with our partners at the City’s Department of Transportation (DOT), Metropolitan Transportation Authority (MTA) and other agencies, we address and respond to all transportation needs. Each recommendation is a thoughtful response which carefully takes into account the needs of our community’s wonderfully diverse residents and visitors.
